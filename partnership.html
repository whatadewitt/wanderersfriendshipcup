The Gambian Partnership

A celebration of global friendship through sport...

The FDSA Wanderers and the Fredericton Wanderers of Brikama partnership began in March 2011 while Wanderers co-founder Matt DeCourcey was working with the Nova Scotia-Gambia Association (NSGA) -- a leading organization with 30 years of the on-the-ground health-education work in The Gambia.

While working with the NSGA in The Gambia in the winter of 2010-11, DeCourcey developed a close bond with his colleague, NSGA Trainer Jerreh Manneh, and the Brikama football (soccer) team that Manneh managed. Prior to his return to Canada, DeCourcey left his Gambian counterparts with a nominal sum of seed money for their upcoming season, and a promise to work together to provide opportunities for young Gambians to play soccer.

In August 2011, the FDSA Wanderers sent $350 and two sets of Wanderers jerseys to their Gambian partners to help fund their season, and to use for community outreach initiatives with youth.

In July 2012, with the support of Randall Hazlett of Investors Group, the FDSA Wanderers raised $1000 for the inaugural tournament. In February 2013, the Fredericton District Soccer Association donated an additional $500 and sets of jerseys and cleats to the initiative. The McCain New Brunswick Premier Soccer League donated a trophy that will remain in The Gambia for future competitions. Eight under-15 boys teams competed over three weekends in the inaugural Wanderers Friendship Cup May 2013, in Brikama, The Gambia. The young soccer players also took part in health-education programming delivered by the young Peer-Health Educators trained by the NSGA.

“During my time in The Gambia, I asked many people what young Gambian soccer players needed most. The answer was simple. They don’t want better equipment. They don’t need better fields. All young Gambians want is more opportunities for competition and to play.”
Matt DeCourcey

2019 Update

The Wanderers Development Association in the Gambia, led by our friend Jerreh Manneh, has recently completed hosting the 7th edition of The Wanderers Friendship Cup. The association remains as committed as ever to youth development and community improvement through sport. This year’s tournament, in partnership with West Coast Region Football Academies Association and the Gambia Senior Secondary Schools Sports Committee (LOC) hosted the tournament into two regions (West Coast Region and Lower River Region) culminating in the final at the Brikama Mini Stadium. In the future, they hope to expand the tournament into other regions of the Gambia, provide transport for the teams from the remote communities and provide support for coaching education.

Health Education

We are particularly proud of the work being done, in part through our yearly financial commitment to our friends in Brikama, to be able to continue to conduct health education as part of the promoting health lifestyle and lifesaving skills for your people for them to live for a better. This was conducted through Drama and presentation at the football field before and after football matches and they provide training for girls at their various schools on Reproductive Health and Life Skills. The tournament provides young Gambians with live-saving skills and knowledge, enabling them to live healthier and happier lives, as well as providing them with the opportunity to enjoy their right to play. With the ongoing support provided from the Fredericton tournament, the Wanderers Development Association in the Gambia hope to increase educational programs to cover teenage pregnancy and other health issues including drug abuse.
